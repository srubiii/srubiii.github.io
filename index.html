The Global to Grocery Link¶

Sarah Rubi¶

Github Webpage¶

Project Overview:

This project investigates the price correlation between global economic shifts and local retail commerce. It uses global level commodity indices from the World Bank with micro level grocery transactional data. This project aims to show how fluctuations in raw materials influence the prices and sale volumes found in the grocery stores between 2023 through 2025.

Dataset 1: Grocery Store Sales

This dataset from Kaggle includes 1900 rows of data that describes customer behavior as well as prices per unit of each item and how many where purchased. The data is from 2023 to 2025. This data provides the micro level perspective needed for calculating monthly averages and unit prices per category.

Columns include: customer_id, store_name, transaction_date, product_name, quantity, unit_price, total_amount, discount_amount, final_amount, and loyalty_points.

Dataset 2: World Bank Commodity Pricing Intelligence¶

This dataset from Kaggle includes global commodities pricing per month that showcase energy, grains, meats, and other food indices. The data is from 1960 to 2026. This data provides a global prospective to help identify commodity changes which influence consumer prices and retail costs.

Columns include commodities like: coal, oil, natural gas, wheat, sugar, coffee, sugar, metals, rice, meats and many more.

Project Plan:

Hypothesis Questions:

The goal is to invesitgate if global commodities indices affect prices for retail at grocery stores.

Q1: When Oil in the World Bank increases does it correlate to higher unit prices for products in grocery stores?
Q2: Can past commodity prices and past sales data can predict future grocery revenue?
Q3: Will specific increases in commodity prices(ie. grain) affect their specific food retail(ie bread) price by increasing the retail price?

                            (commodity_df["month"] <= "2025-08")]

commodities = [
    # energy / transport
    "Crude oil, average", "Crude oil, Brent", "Crude oil, Dubai", "Crude oil, WTI", "Natural gas, US", "Natural gas index",
    # grains
    "Wheat, US HRW", "Rice, Thai 5%", "Maize",
    # meat / protein
    "Chicken", "Beef", "Fish meal",
    # fruit
    "Banana, US","Orange",
    # sugar / oil / processed food
    "Sugar, world", "Palm oil", "Soybean oil", "Sunflower oil", "Groundnut oil", "Coconut oil",
    # animal feed / dairy proxy
    "Soybeans",
]
    
commodity_small = commodity_df[commodity_df["commodity_name"].isin(commodities)]

commodity_wide = commodity_small.pivot(
    index="month",
    columns="commodity_name",
    values="price_nominal_usd"
).reset_index()

commodity_wide.columns = [
    col.lower().replace(",", "")
    .replace(" ", "_")
    for col in commodity_wide.columns]

commodity_wide = commodity_wide.ffill()

Models:¶

Model 1 - Predict total grocery spending from commodity prices

Can global commodity prices predict how much customers spend at the grocery store?

Commodity prices affect food production cost, transportation cost, and store pricing. If commodity prices increase, grocery price may increase as well, which can change total spending.

Dependent Variable is the final_amount. It represents taht total amount paid for each transaction.

Independent Variables are commodity prices such as banana_us, beef, chicken, coconut_oil, crude_oil_brent, natural_gas_us, orange, palm_oil, maize, rice_thai_5%, soybean_oil, soybeans, sugar_world, sunflower_oil, wheat_us_hrw, crude_oil_master. These variables represent global food and energy prices.

Model Type is a Linear Regression because Dependent and Independent Variables are numeric. Also proves the relationship between the dependent and independent variables.

Model Idea: Add up all independent variables to see if they equal the final_amount. Some possible outcomes include having a positive coefficient which would mean higher commodity prices and higher grocery spending. A negative coefficient which would mean higher prices and less spending. There is also a no effect condition which means no relationship.

Model 2- Predict quantity purchased from commodity prices

Do higher commodity prices reduce how much customers buy?

If commodities become expensive then customers may buy less, choose different products, or may use discounts. So quantity would be influenced by commodity prices.

Dependent variable is quantity. This helps provide the total number of items bought per transaction.

Independent Variables are commodity prices such as banana_us, beef, chicken, coconut_oil, crude_oil_brent, natural_gas_us, orange, palm_oil, maize, rice_thai_5%, soybean_oil, soybeans, sugar_world, sunflower_oil, wheat_us_hrw, crude_oil_master. These variables affect the food cost.

Model Type is a Random Forest. This model can capture nonlinear effects. This model will show that consumers may still buy commodities even if the price has increased, but the consumers still have a price they will not go above. This model will capture that stoppage.

Model Idea: Capture the quantity multplied by the commodity prices. Then analysis the trends whether higher commodity prices correlate to lower quantity bought. No change in prices correlates to stable demand. Some commodity prices increase or decrease may influence the consumer to buy more or less.
