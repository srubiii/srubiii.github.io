The Global to Grocery Link¶

Sarah Rubi¶

Github Webpage¶

Project Overview:

This project investigates the price correlation between global economic shifts and local retail commerce. It uses global level commodity indices from the World Bank with micro level grocery transactional data. This project aims to show how fluctuations in raw materials influence the prices and sale volumes found in the grocery stores between 2023 through 2025.

Dataset 1: Grocery Store Sales

This dataset from Kaggle includes 1900 rows of data that describes customer behavior as well as prices per unit of each item and how many where purchased. The data is from 2023 to 2025. This data provides the micro level perspective needed for calculating monthly averages and unit prices per category.

Columns include: customer_id, store_name, transaction_date, product_name, quantity, unit_price, total_amount, discount_amount, final_amount, and loyalty_points.

Dataset 2: World Bank Commodity Pricing Intelligence¶

This dataset from Kaggle includes global commodities pricing per month that showcase energy, grains, meats, and other food indices. The data is from 1960 to 2026. This data provides a global prospective to help identify commodity changes which influence consumer prices and retail costs.

Columns include commodities like: coal, oil, natural gas, wheat, sugar, coffee, sugar, metals, rice, meats and many more.

Project Plan:

Hypothesis Questions:

The goal is to invesitgate if global commodities indices affect prices for retail at grocery stores.

Q1: When Oil in the World Bank increases does it correlate to higher unit prices for product in grocery stores?
Q2: Past commodity prices and past sales data can predict future grocery revenue
Q3: Specific commodity prices(ie. grain) increase will affect their specific food retail(ie bread) price by increasing retail price.

#all NaN are going to be 'Unknown'
commodity_df = commodity_df.fillna("Unknown")

commodity_df["date"] = pd.to_datetime(commodity_df["date"])

commodity_df["month"] = commodity_df["date"].dt.to_period("M")

#only want dates from august 2023 to august 2025 to match dataset 1
commodity_df = commodity_df[(commodity_df["month"] >= "2023-08") &
                            (commodity_df["month"] <= "2025-08")]

commodities = [
    # energy / transport
    "Crude oil, average", "Crude oil, Brent", "Crude oil, Dubai", "Crude oil, WTI", "Natural gas, US", "Natural gas index",
    # grains
    "Wheat, US HRW", "Rice, Thai 5%", "Maize",
    # meat / protein
    "Chicken", "Beef", "Fish meal",
    # fruit
    "Banana, US","Orange",
    # sugar / oil / processed food
    "Sugar, world", "Palm oil", "Soybean oil", "Sunflower oil", "Groundnut oil", "Coconut oil",
    # animal feed / dairy proxy
    "Soybeans",
]
    
commodity_small = commodity_df[commodity_df["commodity_name"].isin(commodities)]

commodity_wide = commodity_small.pivot(
    index="month",
    columns="commodity_name",
    values="price_nominal_usd"
).reset_index()

commodity_wide.columns = [
    col.lower().replace(",", "")
    .replace(" ", "_")
    for col in commodity_wide.columns]
